Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about bookkeeping and tax services in South Africa

What tax returns do I need to submit in South Africa?

In South Africa, you typically need to submit:

  • Income Tax Return (ITR12) - For individuals and sole proprietors
  • Company Income Tax Return (IT14) - For registered companies
  • VAT Returns (VAT201) - If your turnover exceeds R1 million
  • PAYE Returns (EMP201) - If you have employees
  • Provisional Tax Returns - For businesses with taxable income

When are the tax filing deadlines in South Africa?

Key tax deadlines in South Africa:

  • Individual Tax Returns: 23 October (manual) / 24 November (eFiling)
  • Company Tax Returns: 12 months after financial year-end
  • VAT Returns: 25th of the month following the VAT period
  • PAYE Returns: 7th of the month following the pay period
  • Provisional Tax: 31 August and 28 February

Do I need to register for VAT in South Africa?

VAT registration is mandatory in South Africa when:

  • Your taxable turnover exceeds R1 million in any 12-month period
  • You expect to exceed R1 million in the next 12 months
  • You acquire a business as a going concern

Voluntary registration is possible if your turnover is between R50,000 and R1 million.

What records must I keep for SARS compliance?

SARS requires you to keep the following records for 5 years:

  • Financial Records: Invoices, receipts, bank statements
  • Tax Records: VAT returns, PAYE certificates, tax calculations
  • Business Records: Contracts, agreements, correspondence
  • Asset Records: Fixed asset registers, depreciation schedules
  • Employment Records: Salary records, IRP5 certificates

How much does bookkeeping cost in South Africa?

Bookkeeping costs vary based on your business size and complexity:

  • Small Business (R0-R2M turnover): R800-R2,500 per month
  • Medium Business (R2M-R10M turnover): R2,500-R5,000 per month
  • Large Business (R10M+ turnover): R5,000-R15,000 per month
  • Additional Services: VAT returns, payroll, tax returns extra

We offer competitive rates and can provide a custom quote based on your needs.

What are the penalties for late tax submissions?

SARS penalties for late submissions include:

  • Late Filing: R250 per month (up to R16,000)
  • Late Payment: 10% penalty on outstanding amount
  • Interest: Prime rate + 3% on outstanding amounts
  • Understatement Penalty: 25-200% of tax shortfall
  • Criminal Charges: For serious non-compliance

Professional bookkeeping helps avoid these costly penalties.

Can I claim business expenses against my tax?

Yes, you can claim legitimate business expenses including:

  • Operating Expenses: Rent, utilities, office supplies
  • Vehicle Expenses: Fuel, maintenance, insurance (business use only)
  • Technology: Computers, software, internet costs
  • Professional Services: Legal fees, accounting fees, consulting
  • Marketing: Advertising, website costs, business cards
  • Travel: Accommodation, meals (business-related)

Keep detailed records and receipts for all claims.
